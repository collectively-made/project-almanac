# Weather Prediction

## Reading Clouds

Cloud types indicate coming weather patterns. Cirrus clouds (thin, wispy, high altitude) appearing from the west often precede a warm front and rain within 24-48 hours. Cumulus clouds (puffy, flat-bottomed) indicate fair weather when small and scattered, but when they grow tall into cumulonimbus towers, thunderstorms are developing — seek shelter. Stratus clouds (uniform gray layer) bring steady drizzle or light rain. A rapid lowering of cloud ceiling combined with increasing wind suggests an approaching storm system.

## Wind and Pressure Indicators

Without instruments, observe natural pressure indicators. Smoke rising straight up indicates high pressure and fair weather. Smoke that hangs low and spreads horizontally suggests low pressure and approaching poor weather. Campfire flames that burn steadily indicate stable air; flickering and irregular flames suggest changing conditions. Sound carries farther in low pressure — if distant sounds seem unusually clear, a storm may be approaching. Scent also travels more in low pressure — if you can smell distant rain, mud, or vegetation more than usual, weather is changing.

## Animal Behavior

Animals are sensitive to barometric pressure changes. Birds flying high indicate fair weather; birds roosting early or flying low suggest approaching storms. Insects fly lower in humid, low-pressure air — if fish are jumping more than usual, they are feeding on low-flying insects before a storm. Ants build up the walls of their mounds before rain. Frogs croak more loudly and frequently before storms. Cattle and horses often lie down before rain. These indicators are not perfectly reliable individually, but multiple animal behavior changes occurring together strongly suggest weather change.

## Sky Color Indicators

"Red sky at night, sailor's delight. Red sky in morning, sailor take warning." This adage has meteorological basis. In temperate latitudes, weather generally moves west to east. A red sunset means dust particles are illuminated in dry air to the west — fair weather is approaching. A red sunrise means the fair weather has passed to the east, and moisture-laden air is approaching from the west. A greenish sky color before a storm indicates large hail within the storm cloud. A gray morning sky often clears by afternoon; a gray evening sky typically brings rain overnight.

## Lightning Distance Calculation

Count the seconds between seeing lightning and hearing thunder. Divide by 5 to estimate the storm's distance in miles (divide by 3 for kilometers). Sound travels approximately 1 mile every 5 seconds. If the count is 30 seconds or less (6 miles), you are in the danger zone and should seek shelter immediately. Lightning can strike up to 10 miles from a storm. If caught in the open, avoid hilltops, isolated trees, water, and metal objects. Crouch low on the balls of your feet with your feet together — minimize your contact with the ground and your height. Do not lie flat — this increases your ground contact area.
